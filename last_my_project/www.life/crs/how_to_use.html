You Are In Control, Not Your DNA

Your DNA can tell you what types of exercise cause you to burn fat, or where your strengths and weaknesses lie. However, you are still in control. Just because your DNA makes you better at endurance running doesn’t mean you have to become a marathon runner. On the flip side, you may have great endurance genetics, but you simply hate running.

These genetic traits don’t determine your future. Use this information to create a lifestyle and exercise routine that helps you achieve your goals while minimizing the resistance you face from your genetics. You’ll be able to accomplish more in less time by working with your DNA, instead of living in the dark.

How to Read Your Report

Your genetics can be incredibly complicated, but we’ve made it easy to understand how your genes impact your health and fitness. For each gene, you have two “options,” known as “alleles.” If both alleles are associated with a particular trait, you’ll have a stronger likelihood of experiencing that trait.

If only one allele is related to that trait, you may have a lessened response. But many traits are determined not only by the two alleles in one gene, but by many different alleles on many different genes. Some of your genetic traits will appear as a percentage, where all the different genes have been added up to give you a unique score for that trait.
